Short Bio

Since August 2017, I have been a PhD student in Smead Department of Aerospace Engineering Sciences , University of Colorado Boulder , supervised by Professor Jade Morton . My research interests are applications of machine learning to GNSS (Global Navigation Satellite System)-related remote sensing tasks.

Before that, I worked as a Research Associate in Rolls-Royce@NTU Corporate Lab, Nanyang Technological University from 2015 to 2017, supervised by Professor Sinno Jialin Pan . The research topic includes graph-based deep learning for malware detection and inverse modeling for engine design.

From 2013 to 2015, I was a MEng (Master of Engineering) student in the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Nanyang Technological University who graduated on July 2015. I worked on USRP-Based ( Universal Software Radio Peripheral) OFDM Radar System for Indoor Mapping with Professor Yong Liang Guan and Professor Dmitriy Garmatyuk. Meanwhile, I was also in charge of the demo part of “Distributed MIMO Relaying for Range Extension and Diversity Enhancement” project in Advanced Communications Research Program, supervised by Professor Yong Liang Guan.

Prior to this, I was an undergraduate student in Yingcai Experimental School , University of Electronic Science and Technology of China from 2009 to 2013. During the first three years, I tasted the research at the very first time under Professor Gang Wu’s supervision.

Meanwhile, from July 2012 to June 2013 (forth year of my undergraduate study), I was a research assistant (exchange student) supervised by Professor Yong Liang Guan and Professor Dmitriy Garmatyuk in the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering ,Nanyang Technological University. My research topic included USRP-Based OFDM Radar System for Indoor Mapping and Oversampled Frequency Domain MMSE Equalization for SC-FDMA Communication in Non-Ideal Wireless Channels（which is my bachelor thesis).
