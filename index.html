Short Bio

I am now a first-year Master student in the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Nanyang Technological University. I am working on USRP-Based ( Universal Software Radio Peripheral) OFDM Radar System for Indoor Mapping with Professor GUAN YONG LIANG and Professor Dmitriy Garmatyuk.

I was an undergraduate student in University of Electronic Science and Technology of China from 2009 to 2013. During the first three years, I tasted the research at the very first time under Professor Wu Gang’s supervision.

Meanwhile, from July 2012 to June 2013 (forth year of my undergraduate study), I was a research assistant (exchange student) supervised by Professor GUAN YONG LIANG and Professor Dmitriy Garmatyuk in the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering ,Nanyang Technological University. My research topic included USRP-Based OFDM Radar System for Indoor Mapping and Oversampled Frequency Domain MMSE Equalization for SC-FDMA Communication in Non-Ideal Wireless Channels（which is my bachelor thesis).

Before that, I was a lovely boy living in my hometown ChengDu enjoying basketball and video games.

Although being a Wireless Communications major both for bachelor and master degrees, I didn’t have a clear view that which major I would enjoy. Therefore I made a decision that pursuing a Master Degree as a buffer and expected I would find out what I like. It seems to work right by now(2013)! Wonderful, right?
