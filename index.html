Short Bio

I’m now a Research Associate in School of Computer Engineering, Nanyang Technological University, supervised by Professor Sinno Jialin Pan . My current research topic is deep learning for malware detection.

I was a MEng (Master of Engineering) student in the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Nanyang Technological University who graduated on July 2015. I worked on USRP-Based ( Universal Software Radio Peripheral) OFDM Radar System for Indoor Mapping with Professor Yong Liang Guan and Professor Dmitriy Garmatyuk. Meanwhile, I was also in charge of the demo part of “Distributed MIMO Relaying for Range Extension and Diversity Enhancement” project in Advanced Communications Research Program, supervised by Professor Yong Liang Guan.

Prior to this, I was an undergraduate student in Yingcai Experimental School , University of Electronic Science and Technology of China from 2009 to 2013. During the first three years, I tasted the research at the very first time under Professor Gang Wu’s supervision.

Meanwhile, from July 2012 to June 2013 (forth year of my undergraduate study), I was a research assistant (exchange student) supervised by Professor Yong Liang Guan and Professor Dmitriy Garmatyuk in the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering ,Nanyang Technological University. My research topic included USRP-Based OFDM Radar System for Indoor Mapping and Oversampled Frequency Domain MMSE Equalization for SC-FDMA Communication in Non-Ideal Wireless Channels（which is my bachelor thesis).

Before that, I was a lovely boy living in my hometown ChengDu enjoying basketball and video games.

Although being a Wireless Communications major both for bachelor and master degrees, I didn’t have a clear view that: which field I would enjoy. Therefore I made a decision that I would pursue a Master Degree as a buffer and expected then I would find out what I like. Luckily, now I find it!
